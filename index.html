Riccardo Chioetto

Graduating student in Computer Science and Engineering at Politecnico di Milano

Gamer since I was a child, TV series addict and horror movie lover. I consider myself a determined, ambitious and perfectionist person as well as a typical lone wolf.

My passion for movies and videogames has fueled over the years the desire to become a creator and no longer just a simple spectator, for this reason I have chosen to embark on a personal and university path that would bring me closer to the world of Game Design.

In 2020 I started the development of The Timeless Child, a videogame originally born for a university course, then presented at the 2021 edition of Milan Games Week and finally released for free on Steam the same year.

This experience, in addition to allowing me to interface for the first time with the creative process of a videogame and the leadership of a large and heterogeneous team, has managed to clarify my aspirations for the future: keep making games able to entertain, surprise and excite.

Xcape Room

Final project for the Advanced User Interfaces course at Politecnico di Milano. It is a mixed reality videogame for the Crypt of San Sepolcro in Milan.

The aim was to propose a playful and technological alternative to the traditional visit of the crypt through the creation of a cultural Escape Room.

Developed in a 3-person team, my role was to coordinate the work of the other group members, manage the Unity and GitHub project, design and program puzzles and create 2D and 3D assets.

Technologies employed include:

  • Unity: the graphic engine used for the application development
  • MRTK: set of components and features that accelerate the development of cross-platform mixed reality apps in Unity
  • NRSDK: framework for creating augmented reality experiences for AR glasses on Android
  • Immersal: a framework for mapping real environments and locating users within them
  • Nreal light: glasses for mixed reality

The Timeless Child - Prologue

Videogame originally born as a demo for the course of Online Game Design held by Università degli Studi di Milano in collaboration with Politecnico di Milano, was later expanded and converted into a complete gaming experience.

It is a 2-player cooperative online videogame with first-person perspective, developed by 2 people with the free collaboration of 17 volunteer artists. My role within the team was to supervise and coordinate the work of the artists, collaborate in the design and programming of puzzles and game mechanics and write the premise, the plot, the world-building and the character dialogues.

The game participated in the 2021 edition of Milano Games Week and was later released on Steam, immediately receiving great appreciation from the public and over 25,000 downloads in the first month.

Bachelor degree project for the Computer Engineering course at Politecnico di Milano. It is a multiplayer online videogame inspired by the board game Santorini.

Developed by 3 people, my role was to coordinate the work of the other team members, design and program the game mechanics and build the 3D environment.

The game was created using Java as the programming language, the MVC pattern and JavaFX for the graphics. The interaction is twofold: the user can play either through the command line interface (CLI) or through the graphical interface (GUI).
